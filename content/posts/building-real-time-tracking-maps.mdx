Live tracking looks easy until the GPS signal gets noisy. Raw telemetry arrives at
irregular intervals, sometimes jumps, and occasionally stalls. If you bind markers
directly to incoming packets, the map jitters and teleports.

## The problem

A naive pipeline does `socket.on('telemetry', p => marker.setPosition(p))`. Under
unstable GPS you get:

- **Jitter** — tiny position deltas every frame.
- **Teleports** — a late packet snaps the marker across the screen.
- **Stalls** — no packets for a second, then a burst.

## Adaptive buffering

Instead of rendering each packet immediately, buffer briefly and replay on a fixed
render clock. The buffer size adapts to the observed packet rate: larger when the
stream is bursty, smaller when it's steady — trading a little latency for stability.

## Position smoothing

Between known points, interpolate toward the next target rather than snapping. A
simple critically-damped easing makes movement feel continuous without lagging far
behind reality.

The result: a map that stays readable for operators even when the underlying signal
is far from perfect.
